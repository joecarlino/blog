Starting my journey at Devschool

There is a fundamental difference between sitting here and copying from a book, and actually understanding what is going on. If you are essentially new to coding like myself, you will pick up on this very quickly.

I am currently going through a book (mentioned in a previous blog post) that has me copying and pasting a lot of code, with the eventual goal of creating a functional online store. It takes for granted that you have a significant amount of coding experience, however you can just copy the code and it will work just fine.

Let me say this as bluntly as possible: Doing that, in my opinion, is fucking pointless.

I’ve been supplementing my book with classes on codeschool and code academy, along with a healthy dose of web searches to understand every function as we go along. Granted, my progress in the book has been “slow” but I know what I’m doing, and I won’t go any further until I know what is happening. I’ve had a really nice refresher on CSS as well, and it was great to be working with something I’m at least somewhat familiar with.

I’ve also been honing up on Vim. I’ll admit, I was a bit skeptical at first. When I first started, I was coding on cloud 9 , which served its purpose for me as a novice user. As I have progressed in Vim, I’ve found myself able to move around much faster, and without having to deal with some of the issues I saw with a browser based IDE (lag, mainly)

So, progress is good. Hopefully, tomorrow I will start a new project. I’m excited, and as motivated as ever.
